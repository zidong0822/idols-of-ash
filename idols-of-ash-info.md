# Idols of Ash Comprehensive Guide

## 1. Game Overview
- **Game Name:** Idols of Ash
- **Developer:** Leafy Games
- **Genre:** First-person horror / Parkour action
- **Core Gameplay:** Defined as a "movement-shooter without shooting", the game features no combat or firearms. Your only tool is a high-tech grappling hook, and your only option for survival is to escape.

## 2. Theme & Background Setting
Players awaken in a decaying ancient structure, thousands of meters below the surface. There is no sun, no escape route, only broken stone corridors, deep abysses, and unsettling darkness. 
Your mission is to descend as deep as possible to the very bottom of the structure. However, there is something huge, multi-legged, and incredibly hungry lurking in the darkness—referred to by the community as the **"Murderpede"**. This creature never sleeps and only stops hunting when you are dead. The game relies heavily on real-time tension, momentum, and atmospheric elements to deliver intense psychological horror.

## 3. Game Modes
The game offers three different modes tailored to various skill levels:
- **Normal Mode (Moderate):** A balanced 20-30 minute run, perfect for beginners looking to learn the game mechanics and movement skills.
- **Nightmare Mode (High):** Designed for hardcore and experienced gamers. This mode significantly increases enemy speed and introduces much more challenging jumps and map layouts.
- **The First Kiln (Extreme):** An insane challenge where players face hordes of enemies along with hidden map items for true masters to collect.

## 4. Mechanics & Controls
The core movement system is famously known as "Schmovement", which seamlessly combines swinging, grappling, and mid-air control.
- **W / A / S / D:** Basic movement
- **Mouse:** Aim / Look / Rotate view
- **Left Click (Hold):** Launch and hold the grappling hook
- **Spacebar:** Jump
- **Left Shift:** Sprint (useful when running on the ground)
- **E:** Interact with doors and switches
- **ESC:** Open settings menu (to adjust mouse sensitivity, etc.)

## 5. Walkthrough & Survival Tips

### 1) The 45-Degree Rule
When swinging on the grappling hook, pay close attention to your body angle. Releasing the hook when your body forms a **45-degree angle to the vertical** will launch you the furthest and grant maximum momentum.
- **Releasing too early:** You will lose height and fall.
- **Releasing too late:** You will hit the wall and lose all your speed.

### 2) Sound is the Map (Listen Closely)
Audio cues often alert you to danger before visuals do:
- **Soft, steady chirping/friction:** The Murderpede is far away. You can safely focus on finding your path.
- **Loud, rapid, high-pitched noise:** The Murderpede is charging straight at you from behind. Pick up speed immediately!
- **Sudden silence after a hiss:** Extremely dangerous. The creature is either directly above you or about to pounce.
- **Acoustic Navigation:** If you are lost, rotate your view and listen for echoes. The clearest sound usually marks the direction of the abyss and the next grab point.

### 3) No Looking Back
Turning your camera around to look at the monster mid-swing will mess up your hook's inertia and significantly slow you down. Trust your ears and keep moving forward.

### 4) Never Stop Mid-Game
If you stand completely still in an open area for more than 3 seconds, the game triggers a "hunt" event, causing the centipedes to rush you at double speed. If you must pause, do so under bridges or near large stone pillars.

### 5) Locate the Aqueduct (Hidden Shortcut)
Near the center pillar in the mid-game, there is a hidden portal that leads down to flooded levels. Taking this route can shave 30% off your run time. However, the water obstructs your vision, and sounds travel further in water, making you much easier for the Murderpede to target.
